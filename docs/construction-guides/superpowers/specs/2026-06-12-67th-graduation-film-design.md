# 67th Graduation Film Design

## Goal

Use the material under `C:\Users\user\Downloads\微電影素材\_整理後` and the
VIDEO PIPELINE to produce an energetic, story-driven graduation film of at
least ten minutes.

## Creative Direction

The central story is:

> A group of strangers enters the training center, learns to carry risk and
> responsibility together, and leaves as colleagues ready to protect the power
> system.

Narrative sections use spoken Mandarin voice-over and longer visual holds.
Course-introduction sections use rhythmic MV editing, concise course labels,
music-driven cuts, and selected location sound. The film should feel like a
movie rather than a course presentation.

## Structure

Target duration: 11-13 minutes.

| Time | Chapter | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| 00:00-01:15 | First Arrival | Drone, entrance, daily-life details, voice-over |
| 01:15-02:15 | The Promise | Safety meeting, group images, location sound |
| 02:15-04:45 | First Battle: Fundamentals | Pole work, insulator cleaning, live-line work, fast MV |
| 04:45-06:45 | Second Battle: Field Practice | Safety simulation, cable pulling, tower training, fast MV |
| 06:45-08:15 | More Than Training | Sports, birthdays, blood donation, daily life, bright MV |
| 08:15-09:10 | Choosing the Future | Mandatory preference-selection footage, director remarks, voice-over |
| 09:10-10:15 | Gratitude and Farewell | Teacher thanks, group images, memories |
| 10:15-11:30+ | Departure | Tower and drone images, final voice-over, energetic finish |

## Material Strategy

- Prefer original categorized footage and photos.
- Include preference-selection footage marked as mandatory.
- Use the existing 67th graduation film only as a bridge for course items that
  are absent from the original categorized material.
- Do not represent missing course footage as a newly captured event.
- Preserve useful location sound for chants, cheering, instruction, and
  director remarks.
- Turn still photos into restrained push, pan, detail, or montage shots.

## Editorial Rules

- Course MV shots generally hold 1.5-3.0 seconds and cut on action or music.
- Narrative shots generally hold 3-7 seconds to leave room for voice-over.
- Avoid presentation-style enumeration. Group courses into dramatic chapters.
- Use chapter cards and short course labels only when they improve orientation.
- Use a steady Taiwan Mandarin male voice for narration.
- Add Traditional Chinese subtitles for narration.
- Build emotional contrast: anticipation, pressure, momentum, camaraderie,
  reflection, and release.

## Audio Design

- Opening: restrained cinematic pulse.
- Course chapters: fast, percussive inspirational music.
- Daily-life chapter: brighter and warmer music.
- Farewell: music drops back under narration, then rises for the final montage.
- Location sound punctuates major transitions without making dialogue unclear.

## Deliverables

- `final.mp4`, at least ten minutes long.
- Narration audio and Traditional Chinese subtitles.
- VIDEO PIPELINE SPEC, BUILD, and VERIFY artifacts.
- Keyframe grid, timeline audit, caption audit, visual audit, and verification
  result.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Duration is at least 600 seconds.
- The opening and ending are story-driven and narrated.
- Course introductions are presented primarily as MV sequences.
- The film includes safety, fundamental skills, field practice, extracurricular
  life, preference selection, director remarks, gratitude, and departure.
- The result has no missing media, broken audio, unreadable subtitles, or
  obvious black-frame gaps.
