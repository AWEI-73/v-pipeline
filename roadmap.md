# V Pipeline — Human Roadmap

This is a compact product-direction summary for the owner. It is not an agent
boot document, run-state authority, execution plan, or capability registry.

Agents start at `RUNBOOK.md`, read the current pointer in
`HANDOFF_CURRENT.md`, then load only the relevant skill and contract. Do not
preload this roadmap or its archived history into routine task context.

The full pre-consolidation roadmap is preserved at
`docs/archive/roadmap-snapshots/2026-07-20-full-roadmap.md`.

## Product direction

V Pipeline is an agentic long-form video-production system. Its purpose is to
turn ambiguous intent and mixed media into a reviewable, traceable candidate
without pretending that technical correctness equals creative quality.

The product is intentionally split into three responsibilities:

- Orchestrator: resolves ambiguity, owns cross-stage state, and routes work.
- Worker/factory: performs bounded deterministic or registered capability work.
- Human owner: decides facts, taste, creative approval, and delivery.

The Editorial Reviewer is a read-only cross-cutting role. It reuses persisted
eye/ear/heart evidence, records strengths and evidence-bound findings, and
routes proposals to existing capabilities. It does not repair, render, mutate
canonical state, approve creative quality, or claim delivery.

## Canonical lifecycle

The lifecycle remains Stage 0–10:

1. Video Intent Planner
2. Story / structure clarification
3. Material Truth
4. Coverage and decision gate
5. Layered edit planning
6. Official render
7. Verify and editorial review
8. Owner/workbench review
9. Brownfield / finishing revision
10. Delivery

Editing loops are methods used inside the lifecycle. They do not create a
second pipeline or advance the Stage cursor.

## Current strengths

- One operational entry and machine-readable current handoff.
- Registered tools, capability ownership, no-orphan audits, receipts, hashes,
  and fail-closed evidence binding.
- Material Map, picture planning, subtitle binding, audio planning, effects,
  rendering, verification, and Brownfield return routes exist as real tools.
- Uniform 0.5-second timeline walls let a strong reviewer inspect long-form
  structure with stable coordinates and reusable evidence.
- CapCut can be used as an external finishing backend after intent-bearing
  picture/text decisions are locked; local renderers remain fallback.

## Current limits

- Picture-driven long-form story quality still depends on upstream material
  truth, owner intent, and reviewer judgment.
- Technical QA can prove that a candidate is healthy and contract-aligned; it
  cannot prove that the film is moving, elegant, or worth delivering.
- Unannotated or previously curated source material imposes a real semantic
  ceiling. The system must expose that ceiling rather than invent certainty.
- Effects and music are partly taste-crystallized capabilities; mature external
  tools and licensed libraries often have a higher ceiling than local bespoke
  implementations.

## Near-term priorities

1. Finish the first long-form closure with complete receipt, finding-ledger,
   verification-state, owner-verdict, and delivery evidence.
2. Calibrate the Editorial Reviewer against owner findings and measure useful
   finding rate, proposal adoption, and noise.
3. Make machine receipts a normal tool output on the highest-value render,
   mix, review, and verify surfaces.
4. Prove transfer on a second, better-annotated long-form material set and
   measure marginal time, agent turns, renders, and human review minutes.
5. Keep external finishing bounded by backend provenance and return every
   candidate through Verify.

## Build / buy boundary

- Intent-bearing truth stays owned by V Pipeline: story, material truth,
  picture lock, approved text, state, verification, and delivery evidence.
- Taste-crystallized execution is buy/borrow first: music libraries, CapCut
  finishing, and mature templates.
- Custom effect work is reserved for film-specific needs that existing tools do
  not express honestly.
